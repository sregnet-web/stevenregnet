Degrees & Certifications

I am currently completing my Bachelor's degree at Arizona State University, with a focus on learning, design, and instructional development. My academic work emphasizes how people learn, how training can be structured for effectiveness, and how to design learning experiences that support real-world performance.

I also hold two Community College of the Air Force (CCAF) degrees: Avionics Systems Technology and Instructor of Technology and Military Science. These programs provided a foundation in both technical systems and instructional methodology, reinforcing my ability to operate as both a subject matter expert and an instructor.

In addition, I hold a General Radiotelephone Operator License (GROL), reflecting my technical background in communications and avionics systems. I continue to pursue professional development opportunities that align with instructional design and training.

Projects & Coursework

My academic work has centered on the design and development of learning experiences that are practical, structured, and performance-focused. Through coursework, I have created training materials, multimedia presentations, and scenario-based learning activities designed to improve engagement and knowledge retention.

A significant portion of my work has focused on instructional design principles, including aligning learning objectives with outcomes, applying evidence-based learning strategies, and designing content for clarity and usability. These projects have allowed me to translate theory into practical application, reinforcing how effective training directly supports performance.

In addition to coursework, I have applied these principles in real-world training environments, developing and refining instructional materials that support both technical and leadership development. This combination of academic work and applied experience has strengthened my ability to design training that is both effective and relevant.
